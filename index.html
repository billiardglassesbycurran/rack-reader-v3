🚀 Quick Start
1️⃣
Take a picture of your table
Tap Start Camera and point at the table, or tap 📁 Upload Photo to pick one from your phone.
2️⃣
Pick your game, then AI Detect
Choose 8-Ball, 9-Ball, or Straight in Step 4 (the rules change which ball you should shoot), then tap 🤖 AI Detect in the bottom bar. The AI finds and marks the balls — tap any it gets wrong to fix it.
3️⃣
Align the table
Tap 📐 Align Table, then tap the table's 4 corners. Every pocket gets placed for you automatically.
4️⃣
Tap Suggest Best Shot
It's in the bar at the bottom of your screen. The app reads the shot, rates how hard it is, and coaches you through it.
💡 That's it! Everything below explains each feature in more detail.
✨ Simple vs Advanced
✨
Simple View — just the essentials
The app always opens in Simple View. It shows only what you need to make the ball: the crowned best shot, the ghost-ball aim ring, and your cue line — a clean read with nothing extra to sort through.
🎛️
Advanced View — the full read
Switch to Advanced for the complete picture: every ball's lines, cut-angle color coding, English (spin) control, coaching notes, combo shots, and all aiming aids. Drop back to Simple any time for a clean table.
🧹
Resets to Simple each visit
The app returns to Simple View every time you open it, so you always start with a clean, uncluttered read. Advanced is a choice you opt into per session.
📷 Getting a Picture
📷
Start Camera
Turns on your camera. Point at the pool table from above (best) or from your playing angle.
📁
Upload a Photo (folder button)
Tap the yellow folder to pick a photo already on your phone — handy for reviewing a shot later or if the lighting is better in a saved picture.
⏱️
Auto-Scan
When on, the app snaps a picture by itself whenever the balls stop moving. Great if your phone is on a stand or tripod.
⚡
Scan Now
Grabs the current camera picture right away, without waiting.
💡 Tripod tip: Mount the phone overhead, turn on Auto-Scan, and the app reads the table every time the balls stop.
📐 Align Table
🔲
Tap the 4 corners
Tap 📐 Align Table, then tap the table's corners in order: top-left, top-right, bottom-right, bottom-left. The app maps the whole playing surface and places every visible pocket automatically — no manual pocket marking needed.
🤏
Fine-tune by dragging
Drag any corner to nudge it — the zoom window helps you land it exactly, and the pockets update as you go.
✂️
Table partly cut off?
Slightly cropped (a corner or rail just off-frame): tap the nearest edge, fine-tune by dragging — only pockets actually in the photo get placed. Only part of the table showing (one end, a pocket or two): skip Align and use ＋ Pocket above the table to drop a pocket on each one you can see.
↔️
Works from any angle
Overhead shots and natural playing-angle shots both work — the box fits whatever shape the table makes in your photo.
🤖 AI Detection
🤖
AI Detect Button
After you have a picture, tap this to automatically find and mark the balls. AI-placed markers show a small purple "AI" tag.
🔄
Re-detect
Not happy with the result? Tap Re-detect to clear the AI markers and try again.
🎚️
AI Confidence Slider
Low (10–30%) finds more balls but may guess. High (60%+) only marks balls it's very sure about.
✋
When the AI gets a ball wrong
The AI sometimes mixes up a solid and a stripe — or even the cue ball — especially at an angle or in tricky lighting. No problem: just tap the ball and it cycles Solid → Stripe → 8-ball → Cue until it's right. No re-scan needed. (The cue slot is skipped if you already have a cue on the table.)
💡 Best results: Shoot from above in good light. If the AI misses balls, lower the confidence slider or mark them by hand.

⚪
Cue Ball
Mark the white ball. The app draws its aim lines starting FROM the cue ball.

📐 Player vs Overhead View
🧍
Player View
For pictures taken from where you stand. Angles are approximate — the camera angle bends the geometry — so advice is a helpful guide, not exact.
🗺️
Overhead View
For pictures taken straight down over the table (phone on a stand above it). This gives precise cut angles and the most accurate geometry.
💡 Want exact angles? Hold the phone above the table and switch to Overhead View.
🎱 English / Spin Control
🔘
Turn it on
Toggle English/Spin Control in settings. A cue ball diagram pops up so you can choose where you'll strike the ball.
🌀
Pick your contact point
Tap a zone: Follow (top), Draw (bottom), Stun (just below center), Left, Right, or Center. Each changes how the cue ball behaves after contact.
➡️
See the path
A colored line on the picture shows where the cue ball travels after it hits, based on your spin choice (shown in Overhead View).
🎯 Aiming Aids
🟡
Target Line
Draws the aim line from your cue, through the contact point, to the pocket.
🎯
Contact Point
A crosshair showing the exact spot where the cue ball will touch the object ball. It explains the cut — but because balls have width, aiming your cue AT this point undercuts every cut shot. Aim at the dotted ghost ring instead; this marker is for understanding, not aiming.
💠
Diamond System (Advanced)
Turns on the classic Corner-5 rail numbers — a learning overlay for kick and bank shots. Numbers follow Dr. Dave’s standard: the corner pocket is 5, and the long rail runs 0–8. The rule of thumb is cue-ball number − target number = the diamond to aim through. Honest caveat straight from the pros: it’s a starting estimate that assumes a rolling cue ball with running English, and every table plays a little differently — feel the table and adjust. Needs a fully aligned table (all four rails in the photo); it won’t show on a partial view.
💡 Turn these on or off anytime in settings. Start with Target Line and Contact Point — they're the easiest to read.
🔍 Shot Suggestions
📐
Cut Angle
0° = straight in (easiest). 90° = maximum cut (hardest). Color coded green / amber / red.
🔢
Contact Aim Key (1–7)
A small ball face with 7 vertical lines shows exactly where to strike the object ball — 4 is dead center, 3/5 a quarter-ball, 2/6 a half-ball, 1/7 a thin edge. The green line is your spot, like the alignment lines on a marked ball.

Tap ✨ Auto next to Align Table and Shot Reader looks for the felt and drops all four corners for you — then just drag any that are off. Works best when the whole table is visible and the felt colour stands out. In camera mode the outline appears live on the video as you frame the table, and every capture auto-aligns itself. 🎓
Try a Sample Shot
No table around? Tap 🎓 Try a Sample Shot on the empty frame — a practice layout loads on a drawn table and the full analysis runs on it: crown, ghost ring, contact key, difficulty. Great for showing a friend what the app does. Load any real photo to leave sample mode.
🚶
Person-in-frame check (camera)
When capturing from the camera, Shot Reader waits if someone is leaning over or walking through the shot — you’ll see “Player in frame” and auto-scan re-arms on its own. People at the edges of the frame (players at the next table) are ignored — only someone over YOUR table blocks. Need the photo anyway? Tap ⚡ SCAN NOW twice — or turn the check off in the camera settings for busy rooms. Uploaded photos are never checked.
🎬
Not for the break shot
Shot Reader analyzes an open table — cue ball, object balls, pockets. It can’t plan the break itself (a racked cluster has no single “best shot”). Break first, then take your picture.
🥇
9-Ball: set your lowest ball
In 9-Ball you must hit the lowest-numbered ball first — and the AI can’t read numbers yet. Tap 🎯 Set Lowest Ball (Step 4), then tap that ball; it gets a cyan LOWEST ring and the suggestion plans it. Skip this and the app shows the easiest shot with a reminder to confirm it’s legal.

🛡️
Safety Suggestion
On really hard shots (7+), the app suggests playing a safety instead — a smart defensive move.

📜 Game Rules
Quick summaries based on WPA World Standardized Rules — house rules vary, so agree before you break.
🎱 8-Ball
Fifteen balls: one player pockets the solids (1–7), the other the stripes (9–15). Groups are decided by the first legally pocketed ball after the break (the table is "open" until then). Pocket your whole group, then legally pocket the 8-ball in a called pocket to win. Pocketing the 8 early, or scratching while shooting the 8, loses the game. On each shot you must hit one of your own balls first and then drive any ball to a rail or pocket one — otherwise it's a foul (cue ball in hand for your opponent in most rule sets).
🔢 9-Ball
Played with balls 1–9. On every shot the cue ball must contact the lowest-numbered ball on the table first — that's the whole game. Balls don't have to drop in order: a combo off the lowest ball counts, and pocketing the 9 legally at any time wins. Fouls (wrong ball first, no rail after contact, scratch) give your opponent ball-in-hand anywhere. Three consecutive fouls loses the rack in most rule sets.
1️⃣4️⃣.1️⃣ Straight Pool (14.1 Continuous)
Every ball counts one point; first to the agreed score (often 100) wins. You must call ball and pocket on every shot. When 14 balls are pocketed, they're re-racked with the 15th left in place and the run continues — big runs are the whole art. Fouls cost a point; three consecutive fouls costs 15 plus a re-rack break.
Shot Reader tip: pick your game in Step 4 first — the rules change which ball you should be shooting at.
💠 How to use the Diamond System
The rails are numbered. For a kick or bank shot, use the rule:
cue-ball # − target # = aim diamond
Find the diamond number nearest your cue ball, subtract the number where you want the ball to arrive, and aim through the diamond that equals the difference.
📎 It's a starting estimate (assumes a rolling cue ball with running English). Every table plays a little differently — feel it out and adjust. Full details in 📖 Guide.
